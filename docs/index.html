Q3: What about the welfare?

Introduction

To assess the welfare, we’d like to catch a glimpse of indicators in two aspects, basically the health system and the tax rate.

Overall Health System Performance

Health is a vital issue throughout one’s life. According to a paper published from World Health Organization (WHO), the health system of 119 countries worldwide has been scored. 1.00 is set as the full mark and the countries are ranked by their scores in descending order.

Health Expenditure

Higher healthcare expenditure usually means better provision of health opportunities. We have three indicators concerning the health expenditure.

Health Insurance

Health insurance is an essential make-up of health system that it provides financial protection in case people have a serious accident or illness. Here is the coverage of health insurance collected by ILO in 2014.

Tax

People pay taxes to fund a variety of federal, state, and local services. Half of Americans’ tax burden is for federal programs. However, taxes can also eat up a considerable portion of people’s salary. We have tax rate in three dimensions to give a sense of how much tax you should pay.

Q5: Will my kids grow up well in this country?

Introduction

Raising kids is a pleasant and meaningful event in one’s life. We illustrate three important stages while raising a kid to indicate whether the candidate countries are suitable for raising kids.

Under Five Mortality Rate

Kids are vulnerable when they are under five, The mortality rate under five indicates how the infrastructure supports the first five years of kids.

Education

Education is essential for children’s mental and physical development. The gross enrollment rate is the ratio of currently enrolled population in the stage of education over the population in suitable ages.

NEET

NEET(Not in Education, Employment, or Training indicate what is the general) provides an index of how teenagers are involved in social engagement after 18, including continuous education, employment, and training.

Conclusion

With these data about children’s development, we can get a sense of how children are grown in the candidate countries.
